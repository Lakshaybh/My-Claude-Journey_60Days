Business Overview

Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) is India's largest IT services company and among the top 3 globally. Founded in 1968, it provides IT services, consulting, and business process solutions to clients across banking, retail, manufacturing, and government sectors. TCS crossed the $30 Billion revenue milestone in FY2025, serving clients across 55+ countries. It is a subsidiary of Tata Sons (Tata Group).

Key verticals: BFSI (~32%), Retail & Consumer (~14%), Communication & Media (~10%), Manufacturing (~10%), Life Sciences (~8%), Technology & Services (~8%).

Fundamental Quality

Overall Fundamental Quality Rating

⭐ STRONG

TCS is a debt-free, cash-generative, dividend-rich IT giant with industry-leading ROE (>50%), ROCE (>54%), healthy margins (~20% NPM), and a globally respected Tata brand. Its scale (₹2.5L Cr revenue, $30 Bn milestone) and client stickiness provide a durable competitive moat. Near-term growth has slowed (3Y revenue CAGR ~4.3%) but profitability remains world-class. Valuation has corrected to multi-year lows (P/E ~22.5x), offering an attractive entry vs historical averages.

3 Key Strengths
💪
Moat & Scale: Largest Indian IT company with $30Bn+ revenue. Switching costs for large enterprise clients are very high — TCS manages mission-critical systems for BFSI, retail, and government clients globally.
💰
Capital Efficiency: ROE of 51% and ROCE of 55% with ZERO debt — extremely rare combination at this scale. Returns ₹45,000+ Cr to shareholders annually (94% payout ratio in FY25).
🏆
Governance & Brand: Zero pledging, 71.7% promoter stake (Tata Sons), consistent 25+ years of profitability, strong employer brand helping attract global talent.
2 Watch Points
⚠️
Growth Slowdown: Revenue CAGR has dropped from ~12% (5Y) to ~4.3% (3Y). BFSI and retail verticals facing discretionary IT spend cuts. AI-led disruption may compress long-term headcount-based billing models.
⚠️
FII Outflow & YTD Correction: FII holding declining for 8 consecutive quarters (-3.7 pp). Stock has corrected -25% YTD vs Sensex +5.2% — signals near-term caution from global funds. Monitor quarterly deal wins TCV closely.
Key Metric to Track

📌 Total Contract Value (TCV) of new deal wins — this is the leading indicator for future revenue growth. Watch for acceleration in deal closures, especially from BFSI and generative AI projects.

📌 Also track: EBIT margin guidance each quarter and USD revenue growth in constant currency.
