Day 4 — Comment on 5 posts in your target space

Visibility compounds through comments faster than through posts alone.

Agree + Add Insight + Ask a Question

Find 5 posts from people in Data Science, Analytics, or GenAI. For each: state genuine agreement with their point, add one specific thing you know or noticed (from your own learning, a cert, a simulation), then close with a real question that invites a reply. Never just "Great post!" — that gets skipped by both the author and anyone reading the comments.

Day 5 — Publish Post #2: lessons-learned / hot take

Show a point of view, not just a status update.

0 / 1300 
Something I didn't expect from doing job simulations (J.P. Morgan Quant Research, Deloitte Data Analytics) as a student: the hardest part was never the technical task. It was translating a messy business question into something a dataset could actually answer.

Every course teaches you the tools — Python, SQL, Power BI. Almost none teach you how to sit with an ambiguous problem before you touch any of them.

That's the gap I'm trying to close right now, alongside learning how LLMs and prompt engineering change what "analysis" even means.

If you're a student in data/AI: what's been the harder skill to build for you — the tools, or the judgment around them?

Day 6 — Engage + send 5 more connection requests

Reply to every single comment on both posts — within the first hour if you can, LinkedIn's algorithm rewards early engagement velocity. Then repeat Day 3's targeting for 5 more people.

Day 7 — Review the numbers

Your current baseline: 45 profile views, 1,085 post impressions, 33 search appearances (past 7 days). Check where each moved.

Profile views — measures how compelling your headline + photo are in search/feed. If this rose, your new headline is earning clicks.
Search appearances — measures whether your keywords (new Skills, headline terms) are getting indexed. A jump here confirms the Skills-section fix worked.
Connection acceptance rate — measures whether your targeting and message template (Day 3) are landing. Low acceptance means retarget or rewrite the message, not send more.
