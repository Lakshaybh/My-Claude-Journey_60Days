# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Lakshay Sharma, a Data Science & AI undergrad targeting internships and network growth on LinkedIn. The page is for his own private use first (review and edit his own audit), but its content is written to be screenshotted, printed, and shared on LinkedIn, so the secondary audience is his LinkedIn network: recruiters, peers, and professionals in data/AI who will see the printed PDF or the summary card.

## Product Purpose

A single-page interactive audit of one real LinkedIn profile: a scored roast of the current profile, a full rewrite of every section, a before/after scorecard, a 7-day activation plan, and a shareable summary card. Success is a document credible enough to post on LinkedIn as proof of a real personal-branding exercise, and a working PDF export button that captures the full audit as a clean, printable report.

## Positioning

The only LinkedIn audit built entirely from one person's real profile data, with zero invented achievements, formatted as a report credible enough to publish.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: Download/print the audit as a PDF (primary); read through and act on the profile edits directly in the page (secondary).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "This person audited their own LinkedIn profile honestly, with numbers to back it up."
- Belief ladder: This is a real profile, not a template → the scoring is specific and evidence-based, not generic advice → the rewrite only uses real credentials, so it's trustworthy → the PDF is worth downloading and posting.
- Proof on hand: the profile content itself (headline, About, experience, certifications) supplied directly by the user; no external files.

## Brand Personality

Confident, sharp, credible. Reads like a consulting deliverable, not a casual blog post or a slide deck. Direct and specific rather than motivational or vague; the tone can be blunt (a roast) but the visual register stays composed and professional throughout.

## Anti-references

Not a SaaS dashboard look (no gradient hero-metric tiles, no glassmorphism). Not a casual listicle or meme-style share card. Avoid anything that reads as "AI-generated pitch deck": no rainbow status colors, no decorative emoji-as-bullets, no stock-photo hero.

## Design Principles

- Report before app: structure, hierarchy, and print output are as important as on-screen interactivity.
- One accent, used with intent: orange marks action and emphasis only, never decoration.
- Evidence over adjectives: every claim ties back to a quoted line, a real score, or a real credential.
- The PDF is a first-class output, not an afterthought bolted onto a web page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA contrast for body text. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keyboard-operable tabs and print button; visible focus states throughout.
