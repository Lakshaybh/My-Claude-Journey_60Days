I asked Claude to interview me before it wrote a single line of a plan. Sixteen questions in, we hadn't touched code once.

Day 51 kicked off my final capstone: 10 days to go from zero idea to a deployed product. My first instinct was to jump straight to features. Claude pushed back and asked what my actual day looks like as a data analyst intern, how many hours I really have (one, most days), and what part of my job is genuinely tedious. The honest answer was writing SQL and DAX queries against schemas I don't fully know.

That single answer became the whole product. We landed on QueryMind: paste a database schema, ask your question in plain English, get back a correct SQL query with a plain-English explanation of what it does. No login, no live execution, no chat back-and-forth. Every time I wanted to add "one more feature," Claude asked whether it earned its place in a one-hour-a-day, nine-day build, and most of the time the answer was no.

By the end of the session I had a full PRD, a day-by-day blueprint through deployment, and a pitch deck, all scoped to what one focused hour a day can actually finish.

The lesson: the right question at the start saves more time than any shortcut later.
