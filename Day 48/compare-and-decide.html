How this was researched

Method

Pricing and feature claims were pulled directly from each vendor's official pricing and integrations pages in July 2026. Ease-of-use figures come from G2 review data. G2's review pages blocked direct automated fetching (HTTP 403), so those four numbers were read from search-engine result snippets of G2 content rather than a live page load. Treat them as directionally accurate, not laboratory-precise.

Price score

Scored on the cheapest annual-billed paid per-seat tier each vendor publishes (ClickUp Unlimited $7, monday.com Basic $9, Notion Plus $10, Asana Starter $10.99, all per user per month). Free plans exist for all four but were not used for scoring since a growing team outgrows them quickly; free-tier limits are listed in the matrix for reference.

Ease of use score

Asana (8.6/10) and ClickUp (8.8/10, reported as 88% on G2) have a directly stated ease-of-use sub-score. Notion and monday.com do not have a confirmed public ease-of-use sub-score, so their scores use each product's overall G2 star rating (Notion 4.6/5, monday.com 4.7/5) converted to a /10 scale as a proxy, flagged as an estimate in the matrix.

Features score

Built from an 8-point checklist (task management, docs/wiki, kanban view, gantt/timeline view, calendar view, automations, templates, AI features) using only capabilities each vendor's own pricing or feature page explicitly states. A tool scores a point per capability regardless of which paid tier unlocks it. Asana scored lower because its pricing page does not describe a dedicated docs product or AI feature the way the other three do.

Integrations score

Vendors describe integration counts in rounded marketing language rather than a precise audited number: Notion "100+", Asana "200+", monday.com "200+", ClickUp "1,000+ tools." ClickUp's figure explicitly combines native integrations, auto-import connectors, and third-party tools in one headline number, which is not directly comparable to the other three vendors' native-integration framing. Third-party marketplace aggregators report different, generally higher counts (for example 454 listings for Asana), but those aren't the vendors' own published claims, so they were excluded from scoring.

Conflicts encountered and how they were resolved

  • ClickUp's ease-of-use figure appeared in sources as both "88%" and "8.5/10" for what is the same underlying G2 dataset. Resolved by using the more frequently cited 88% (8.8/10 equivalent) and noting the discrepancy here rather than silently picking one.
  • Integration totals differ sharply between each vendor's own marketing page and third-party marketplace directories, because directories count every listed third-party connector while vendors round to a headline figure. Resolved by using only the vendor's own stated number for scoring, since that is what a founder will actually see on the pricing page.
  • G2's ease-of-use review pages returned a 403 error to direct automated fetching. Resolved by using search-indexed snippets of the same G2 pages and flagging any number without a directly confirmed sub-score as an estimate rather than presenting it with false precision.
